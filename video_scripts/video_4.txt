2:00-2:30 - Node Pool Optimization
Visual: Node pool comparison. Before: 12 m5.2xlarge nodes, 31% avg utilization. After: 8 m5.xlarge + 4 spot instances, 72% avg utilization. Cost reduction arrow.

Cost: fourteen thousand two hundred dollars per month for the node pool alone. The AI agent recommended a mixed strategy: eight m5 dot xlarge on-demand for baseline workloads, four spot instances for batch and stateless services. Average utilization jumped to seventy-two percent. Monthly node pool cost: six thousand one hundred dollars. Fifty-seven percent reduction. Every deletion goes through a safety check: seven-day grace period, notification to namespace owners, and automatic rollback if a deleted resource is referenced within thirty days.

3:00-3:30 - Guardrails and Safety
Visual: Guardrail policy diagram: Never reduce production below baseline, Always maintain 20% headroom, Require human approval for >50% reduction, Auto-rollback on latency spike.
Voiceover: Safety is non-negotiable. AI agents operate within guardrails you define. Never reduce production resource requests below the ninety-fifth percentile of historical usage. Always maintain twenty percent headroom above peak observed usage. Require human approval for any reduction exceeding fifty percent. Auto-rollback if p99 latency increases more than ten percent after a change. Never delete resources in namespaces tagged production-critical without explicit approval. These guardrails mean the agent optimizes aggressively on staging and development environments — where seventy percent of waste typically lives — while being conservative on production. You get the savings without the risk.
